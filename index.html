A free Skool community for women learning AI from scratch — whether they've got 45 minutes after bedtime or a quiet morning at 58. Beginner-friendly. Judgment-free. No catching up required.

The brand promise: AI is the time-multiplier for women who've never had enough.

It's not "learn AI." It's "AI gives you your hours back so you can finally build the thing." That's a totally different sales pitch — and it's a thousand times more emotional than what most AI-for-beginners communities lead with.

Voice & Tone

How the brand THINKS, SPEAKS, and LEADS. Permission-first, action-second, never coach-on-stage.

THE VOICE FORMULA

Speak directly to HER. Mirror her exact internal thoughts. Use forward-pointing language (becoming, building, beginning). Celebrate her arrival, not her struggle. Hand her permission, generously. End with action, not just understanding.

✓ Always

  • Speak directly to HER (not "people" or "they")
  • Mirror her exact internal thoughts
  • Use forward-pointing language
  • Celebrate her arrival, not her struggle
  • Hand her permission, generously
  • End with action, not understanding alone

✗ Avoid

  • Coach-on-stage language ("I want you to know...")
  • Soft-pedaling or coddling
  • Empty motivation without action
  • Words: still, finally, despite, push through
  • Hustle/grit energy (we're warm, not white-knuckled)
  • Pure black text or pure white backgrounds

⚠️ The "Anyway" Rule — Critical Voice Craft

The word "anyway" can read TWO ways:

✅ Defiant / empowered: "She builds ANYWAY" — confident shrug

❌ Resigned / exhausted: "She builds anyway, *sigh*" — grit-teeth tired

For our exhausted audience, brand voice MUST tilt every "anyway" toward the empowered read by making EVERY surrounding word forward-pointing.

✓ Use (forward-pointing)

  • "You're here. That's the brave part."
  • "AI for women becoming."
  • Words: now, yes, here, today, arrived
  • Celebrate ARRIVAL and POSSIBILITY

✗ Avoid (backward-pointing)

  • "I know you're tired."
  • "AI for women who keep going despite everything."
  • Words: still, finally, despite, even, push through
  • Don't dwell on STRUGGLE

🚫 Words We Never Use

These pull in the wrong direction for our brand voice:

  • Still — implies she's been failing ("she's still trying")
  • Finally — implies she was late ("she finally did it")
  • Despite — centers the obstacle, not her
  • Push through — hustle culture, exhaustion energy
  • Grind — not who we are
  • Struggle — we name it, we don't wallow in it

7-Day Launch Sprint

Open the doors in a week. MVP launch with 2 ignition points live + 6 "Coming Soon" classrooms. Build the rest from inside.

SPRINT STRATEGY

Two ignition point classrooms LIVE (3-6 lessons each). Six core classrooms set up as "Coming Soon" with content drop dates — anticipation, not embarrassment. FireStarter post categories ready with first 5 posts scheduled. Welcome message polished. Day 7: doors open.

1

Skool Setup + Brand the Page

Sign up for Skool's paid plan. Claim URL. Add Mary as admin. Design banner + logo + profile in Canva (Pacific Blue + Coral). Plug in all brand copy: name, tagline, listing description, About section.

2

Build the Structure

Create 2 ignition point classrooms (Stolen Hours + Reclaimed Hours). Create 6 "Coming Soon" core classrooms with content-drop pinned posts. Set classroom navigation order.

3

Schedule the First 5 Posts

Pre-load the first 5 daily prompts so the community feels alive from Day 1.

4

Build Ignition Point Content (Parallel)

Mary: Lessons 1.1–1.3 (Welcome, Stolen Hours System, Bedtime Brain). Kris: Lessons 2.1–2.3 (Welcome, Golden Hours, Tech-Willing).

5

Finish Ignition Point Lessons

Mary: Lessons 1.4–1.6 (Mom Guilt, AI as Co-Founder, Bridge). Kris: Lessons 2.4–2.6 (Your Experience Is the Gold, AI Is Your Secret Weapon, Bridge).

6

Welcome Layer + Polish

Write welcome DM. Pin community guidelines. Test new-member experience as a fake user. Visual polish — every classroom has thumbnail, every FireStarter post category has its emoji + welcome post.

7

🎉 Soft Launch — Open the Doors

Invite 5–10 close friends each. Send personal warm messages. Be present in the community. Reply to every post and comment. Note anything that breaks. Celebrate.
